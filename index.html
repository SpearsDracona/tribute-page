About Akira Toriyama

Akira Toriyama was a manga artist and character designer, best known for his work on Dragon Ball.

He loved to draw from a very young age, and was fascinated as a child by manga and animation.

In 1978, his manga Wonder Island was published in Weekly Sh≈çnen Jump. This was his first published manga.

His first highly successful manga was Dr. Slump, which was serialized in Weekly Sh≈çnen Jump from 1980 to 1984.

In 1983, he created a two-part manga called Dragon Boy, which would eventually evolve to become the serial Dragon Ball. Dragon Ball originally ran from 1984-1995, becoming one of the best-selling manga series of all time.

In 1986, he was hired as a character designer for the role-playing video game Dragon Quest. He would later work on every installment in the Dragon Quest series until his death.

He worked on other video games as well, including Chrono Trigger in 1995, and Blue Dragon in 2006.

His works include many other stories and collaborations with other influential artists.

On March 1, 2024, he passed away from an acute subdural hematoma. He will be missed and his legacy will live on.
